The Building Blocks: Asset Classes Compared

Finding the Right Mix

Each asset class has a unique profile of potential return, risk, and liquidity (how easily it can be turned into cash). A well-built portfolio combines these assets to balance their characteristics. For example, the high growth potential of stocks is often balanced by the stability of bonds.

  • Stocks (Equities): High growth potential, but higher risk.
  • Bonds (Fixed Income): Lower risk and stable income, but lower growth.
  • Real Estate: Can provide income and growth, but is not easily sold (illiquid).
  • Cash: Very safe and liquid, but offers minimal to no growth.

Designing Your Portfolio: 3 Key Principles

1. Asset Allocation

This is the most critical decision: how you split your money between asset classes. The "60/40" portfolio is a classic example.

2. Diversification

The golden rule: "Don't put all your eggs in one basket." Spreading investments reduces the risk of one poor performer sinking your portfolio.
